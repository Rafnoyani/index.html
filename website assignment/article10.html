The Rise of Quantum Computing Explained

Posted on October 8, 2025 by Dr. Alan Turing
AI

Quantum computing has moved from theoretical research to practical applications, with 2025 marking the year when quantum advantage became achievable for specific problems. Here's what this means for technology and society.

1. Quantum Computing Basics

Unlike classical bits, quantum bits (qubits) can exist in superposition, enabling quantum computers to perform many calculations simultaneously. New error-correction techniques have made quantum computers more reliable than ever.

2. Current Applications

Quantum computers are now being used for drug discovery, materials science, and optimization problems. Financial institutions use them for portfolio optimization, while logistics companies solve complex routing problems.

3. Future Potential

Within the next decade, quantum computers may break current encryption standards, necessitating quantum-resistant cryptography. They could also revolutionize artificial intelligence through quantum machine learning algorithms.
